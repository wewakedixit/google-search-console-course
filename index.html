Plain-English glossary

Important words

Click

Someone clicked your website link from Google Search.

Impression

Your website appeared on a Google results page for someone.

CTR

Click-through rate. Clicks divided by impressions.

Average position

The average ranking of your top result for a search.

Indexed

Google knows the page and may show it in search results.

Sitemap

A file that lists pages you want Google to know about.

Examples

How to think like a beginner SEO

Example 1: High impressions, low clicks

A page appears 8,000 times for “family dentist in Delhi” but gets only 40 clicks. This means people are seeing it, but the title or description may not feel useful.

Simple action:

Rewrite the page title to clearly include the service, location, and benefit.

Example 2: New blog is not on Google

You published “How to choose a preschool” yesterday, but it does not show in Google. This can be normal. Google may not have crawled it yet.

Simple action:

Use URL Inspection, test the live URL, and request indexing if the page is ready.

Example 3: Traffic suddenly drops

Your clicks dropped after your website moved from http to https. You might be looking at the wrong property or an old version of the site.

Simple action:

Check that the property matches your live website version exactly.

Example 4: Many pages are not indexed

Search Console says some pages are “Discovered” or “Crawled” but not indexed. Google may know them but has not decided to show them.

Simple action:

Improve weak pages, remove duplicates, and make sure important pages are linked clearly.
